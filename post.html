People tend to seek and remember information that confirms their preexisting beliefs.

By: Jane Doe
June 10, 2022 - 07:23

Confirmation bias is a well-documented psychological phenomenon that refers to the tendency of individuals to seek, interpret, and remember information in a way that confirms their preexisting beliefs or hypotheses while ignoring or discounting information that contradicts those beliefs. In other words, people tend to favor and pay more attention to information that supports what they already believe and dismiss or downplay information that challenges their beliefs.
